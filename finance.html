What is campaign finance?

Running for political office costs money — for ads, staff, travel, and events. To fund their campaigns, candidates set up official committees that accept donations from individuals and organizations. Federal law requires every donation over a small threshold to be publicly reported to the Federal Election Commission (FEC), the independent agency that enforces campaign finance rules.
